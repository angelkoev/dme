How DME Works

DME builds you a personalized workout plan based on your goal, experience level, available equipment, and any injuries — then adjusts future plans based on how your workouts actually go. Here's how to get the most out of it.

1. Create an account

Use Register to create an account, then log in. (If you're just trying things out, ask whoever gave you access about a test account.)

2. Fill in your profile

Go to My Profile and tell DME about yourself:

  • Goal — what you're training for: Strength, Hypertrophy (muscle size), Fat Loss, Endurance, or General Fitness. This decides the shape of your plan — how many sets/reps, how much rest, and whether sessions focus on one muscle group or the whole body.
  • Experience level — Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced. DME won't hand a beginner an advanced-only exercise.
  • Days per week & session length — how often and how long you can train.
  • Where you train and available equipment — DME only picks exercises you can actually do with what you have.
  • Favorite / disliked exercises — favorites get a small boost when DME is choosing between similar options; disliked ones are never picked at all.
  • Preferred / unwanted muscle groups — categories you'd like more of, or want avoided entirely.
  • Injuries — flag a muscle group as injured (with a short note) and DME will never suggest an exercise that targets it.
  • Rest days — days you don't want to train.

You can come back and change any of this later — the next plan you generate will use your latest answers.

3. Generate a plan

On My Dashboard, click "Generate a new workout plan." You can optionally pick a different goal just for that one plan, without changing your saved profile goal. Your plan is made up of sessions (e.g. "Push Day 1"), each with a handful of exercises and their sets, rep range, and rest time.

Generating a new plan replaces your current one — you'll always have exactly one active plan.

4. Follow the plan and log your sessions

After you finish a session, mark it complete on the dashboard: enter how much of it you actually completed (%) and, optionally, a rating. This matters more than it might seem:

  • Exercises you've done recently show up less often, so your plan doesn't get repetitive.
  • Muscle groups you've trained a lot recently get a break, to avoid overtraining.
  • If you're consistently finishing less than 60% of a session, your next plan eases off; finishing over 90% nudges it to get harder.

In short: the more honestly you log, the better your next plan fits you.

5. Track your progress

My Progress shows your current and longest workout streak, any personal records you've set (heaviest weight or most reps on an exercise), and your full workout history.

6. Account settings

You can change your password any time from Change Password.

Browsing without an account

Anyone can browse the full exercise catalog without logging in.
